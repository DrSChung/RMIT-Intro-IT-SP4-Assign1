    Project Idea

    Overview
    The world embraces modern computing and information technology, though miniaturization, sleek interface and sophisticated functionality hinder the already disadvantaged minority with physical and other disabilities from benefiting but fall into oblivion. What supposedly revolutionizing life experience for better in our society is not always materialized in this minority group. More than 2.5M Australian suffer from physical restrictions amongst other disabilities and over 1M of them did not use internet in last 3 months of survey period by Australian Bureau of Statistics [1]. This project is the first phase of my schema to develop, with a pragmatic methodology, highly customizable controller devices as user interface for people with disabilities, and further develop a holistic welcoming service to a better quality and experience in life. My vision: If you can just do a little bit, a lot we will do for you. And the more you can do, the more we can do for you.

    Motivation
    If you have some disabilities and are not able to pour yourself a glass of water, you may feel modern technology difficult to use, if not intimidating. Imagine a technology you have no control of! Feel like losing control of your car in snow? Many Australians suffer from some disabilities and preclude from harnessing modern technology both in therapy and lifestyle choices like others. Modern technology endeavors to embrace everyone.

    Need: People with disabilities have difficulties in controlling and using modern technology. Langan et al [2] concluded that the use of technology is not popular in stroke patients despite its use improved patients' performance and engagement. They are missing out.

    Market Prospect: According to July 2020 updated data revealing the use of technology by people with disability from ABS 2018 Survey of Disability, Ageing and Carers data [1], there are 3,822,600 (~17.7%) of Australian population have disabilities. Of those, 2,529,200 (66.16%) suffer from physical restriction amongst other disabilities, the highest disability incident of the Disability Groups. From the data, 1,088,700 (28.48%) of people with disability did not use internet in last 3 months of survey period. While 15-44 years and 45-64 years age groups are only 0.12% and 5.14% respectively of the 28.48%, 65 years and above is the majority (848,100 or 22.19%).

    Reason: Help them re-integrate to the world. Modern I.T. changes business models and model of living and our lifestyle adapts supposedly for better, e.g. online banking, online shopping, and some home automation. However, some people with disabilities cannot accomplish cumbersome multi-step process, such as 2-factor authentication, ticking 3 or 4 more ultra-small boxes for consent to T&Cs, the company's privacy policies, marketing campaign, confirm correct information. This needs to be modulated.

    Description
    To people with disabilities, modern I.T. is like a double-edged sword. While digitalization, fast processing speed and synchronization, improve motion detection accuracy, and miniaturization transform many equipment from portable to wearable, users with disabilities encounter challenging experience when interacting with these gadgets. The primary goal of this project is to improve user experience and achieve and maintain high participation rate in I.T. use in this cohort. I will design a protocol in evaluating these customers both from medical, functional and I.T. user prospective, collect and analyze the data from the protocol and further develop a pragmatic methodology for future customers.
    The scheme of the protocol as below:
    1. Customer assessment & analysis ‐ analyze customer can & can't do ‐ physical, sensory (including vision and hearing), cognition including problem-solving skills e.g. when encounter a mistake.
    2. Goal settings with customers/families ‐ determine and agree what can/likely be able to achieve with technology use. Outcomes will be in short term: able to use and do things as expected, quality of life improvement; and in long-term: improve ability, maintain I.T. participation, and modify accordingly to improve further.
    3. Hardware prescription ‐ match, modify or design most appropriate control device to suit physical restrictions, try and modify accordingly to achieve good user satisfaction and function. Then match the functionality of selected control device to prospective technology or gadget for trial use and if attain desirable outcomes.
    4. Software prescription ‐ elevate and modify software to suit patient's abilities, such as reducing or bypassing all unnecessary steps.
    5. Testing and accumulating data and experience for future referencing.
    6. Identify determining factors in each step for protocol analysis.
    7. Develop a secure reliable digital platform for our clients (by subscription) so to facilitate simplifying their authentications to other online/digital media.

    Tools & Technology

I consider VVii™ with motion sensors, VVii Switch™ with wearable sensors and controller, XBOX™ with Kinect™, Makey Makey™, Leap Motion Controller™, and variety of joysticks, keyboards, mouse, stylus, cameras and microphones.

    I will need internet access and a range pre-selection of software and programs to assess what the customer can achieve with a selection of control devices. I consider publicly accessible sources such as government websites, internet banking portals for major banks, voice recognition software in social media, as well as commonly used and readily available software programs like word processing. I will also include using media for entertainment and lifestyle matters.

    I will need to set up a database to analysis the protocol for improvement.

    • an occupational therapist to help assessing ADLs and some functional outcome measures;
    • a clinical psychologist to evaluate short-term & long-term user experience and quality of life change;
    • an experienced speech therapist to help determining language ability and deficits in details;
    • an UI & UX designer with wireframing experience and compassion to understand the customer's special needs and to develop customer's user performa;
    • legal advice to consult for relevant legal implications with my products and services.

    Outcomes
    The mission is developing an expert team who knows users with disabilities their issues, limitations and wishes and “prescribe” them appropriate technology suite and set-up for their uses, just like a doctor knows a patient's health problem and prescribe the correct medications with one simple outcome: Better.

    For this project, I evaluate 3 aspects of outcomes:
    1. happy customers
    2. customers maintain use of I.T.
    3. build experience and database from customers and develop an efficient, accurate and reliable technology prescribing methodology for future clients
